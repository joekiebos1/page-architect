# STORYTELLING FOR JIO STORIES

A Jio Story is editorial content. It is not a product page. It does not sell. It does not convert. It builds trust, pride, and belief in Jio's role in India's future — through specific, human, honest storytelling.

The reader arrives curious, not evaluating. The story is the destination. Jio is context, not subject.

---

## What a Jio Story is

A Jio Story documents the real impact Jio has on Indian life — through a product, an initiative, a community, a moment in history, or a person. The subject can be anything. The through-line is always the same: India is changing, and Jio is part of that change.

Stories can be:
- A specific product feature told through the life it enables
- A community or region transformed by connectivity
- A moment in Jio's history that shaped what India's digital life became
- An initiative — education, health, agriculture — where Jio played a role
- A cultural moment where Jio was present

What they are never:
- A product launch announcement
- A feature list dressed as editorial
- A story where Jio is the hero instead of the people it serves

---

## Narrative arc — five acts

### Act 1 — Hook (1–2 blocks)
Open with a specific human moment, a cultural observation, or a vivid scene from Indian life. No brand mention. No product mention. The reader should feel immediately that this story is about something real.

The hook earns the right to tell the rest of the story. If it could have been written by someone who has never been to India, it has failed.

Test: remove the Jio name entirely. Does the opening still make someone want to read on?

### Act 2 — Context (2–3 blocks)
Establish why this moment matters. What was the tension, the gap, the need, the aspiration? This is where India's reality is named honestly — not as a problem to be solved, but as the condition inside which this story takes place.

Jio may be named here, but only as context — not yet as the answer.

### Act 3 — Impact (3–6 blocks)
This is the body of the story. How did things change? What became possible? Who was affected and how? Be specific — name the place, the person, the number, the moment. Generalities are not impact. A specific farmer in Rajasthan who can now check mandi prices is impact. "Millions of farmers benefited" is not.

This is where Jio's role is revealed — not sold. The product or initiative appears as the mechanism, never the point.

### Act 4 — Proof (2–4 blocks)
Specific details that make the impact believable. Numbers, names, real moments. This is where rational credibility is established — not to persuade, but to make the story honest and verifiable.

May include: data, quotes, before/after comparisons, technical decisions that reveal care, historical context.

### Act 5 — Close (1–2 blocks)
A warm, forward-looking conclusion. What does this story mean for India? What comes next? No CTA. No hard sell. The close should feel like the last paragraph of a great piece of journalism — leaving the reader with a feeling, not a task.

End with related stories — three cards connecting to other Jio Stories that extend the reader's journey.

## Tone and voice

**What it sounds like:**
Warm, honest, specific. Proud without being boastful. The voice of someone who genuinely believes in what Jio is doing for India and wants to share it — not the voice of marketing.

**What it does not sound like:**
Corporate press release. Feature announcement. Advertising copy. Vague aspiration. "Empowering" without evidence.

**The India standard:**
Every story must feel grounded in specific Indian reality. Named places, named languages, specific cultural moments, real tensions and real progress. Generic "India" references — street food, cricket, Bollywood as decoration — are not enough. If the story could have been written about any country by someone who has never visited, it has failed.

## Input for a Jio Story

A Jio Story does not start from a product fact sheet. It starts from:

**Story angle** — the specific human moment, initiative, product feature, or historical moment this story is about. One sentence, specific.

**The India context** — which tension or truth from Indian life does this story live inside? (connectivity, aspiration, family, celebration, digital access, language, community)

**The specific evidence** — what are the real, specific details that make this story true and believable? Numbers, places, people, moments. The more specific, the better the story.

**Jio's role** — how did Jio contribute? What product, initiative, or decision made this possible? This should be secondary to the human story — the mechanism, not the hero.

## Cross-linking in Jio Stories

Cross-links in a Jio Story are editorial extensions — an invitation to learn more, never a sales move. They appear when the story naturally references something the reader might want to explore further.

**Two types of cross-link:**

**Product cross-link** — when the story mentions a Jio product as the mechanism behind the impact, link to the product page at that moment. The link should feel like a footnote, not a CTA. The reader who wants to know more can follow it. The reader who does not is not interrupted.

Example: a Jio Story about rural connectivity mentions JioAirFiber — link to the JioAirFiber product page at the point where JioAirFiber is named, not at the end as a call to action.

**Story cross-link** — when the story connects to a broader theme covered by another Jio Story, link to that story. These live in the related-stories block at the end, but may also appear inline when the connection is strong enough to warrant it.

**Rules:**
- Maximum two inline cross-links per story — more feels like advertising
- Inline cross-links appear only in the Impact or Proof sections — never in the Hook or Context, where the brand has not yet been introduced
- The related-stories block at the end is always present and is not counted against the two-link limit
- Links to product pages use the product graph — only link to products that exist in the Jio ecosystem, never invent a destination
- The link label should be the product name or story title — never "click here", "learn more", or "explore"

**What cross-linking is not:**
- A CTA at the end of the story pushing the reader to buy
- A promotional insert that interrupts the narrative
- A reason to mention a product that is not genuinely relevant to the story

---

## What a strong Jio Story is not

- It is not a product page that starts with a human story and ends with a CTA
- It is not a press release formatted as editorial
- It is not a story where Jio is the subject instead of the context
- It is not a collection of impressive numbers without a human moment to anchor them
- It is not vague Indian sentiment — warmth without specificity is decoration, not storytelling
